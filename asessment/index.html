H1 to H5 are heading tags used in HTML to structure web content. They differ in terms of their semantic meanings and hierarchical importance. H1 represents the main heading of a page, while H2 to H5 represent subheadings of decreasing importance. Each heading tag provides context and helps organize the content for better readability and accessibility.

  • H1 is used to represent the main heading of a webpage. It's typically the most important heading on the page and helps users understand the main topic or purpose of the content.
  • H2 is used as a subheading in HTML. It represents a heading of lesser importance compared to H1
  • H3 is often used within sections or subsections to provide additional context or details.
  • H4 is often used within sections or subsections to provide additional levels of hierarchy and clarity to the information presented.
  • H5 is a heading tag in HTML that is used to represent subheadings of even lesser importance compared to H4.
